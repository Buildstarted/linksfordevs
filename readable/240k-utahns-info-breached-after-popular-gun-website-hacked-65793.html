240K Utahns info breached after popular gun website hacked

by https://www.facebook.com/kutv2news
Reading time: 11-14 minutes
Posted here: 18 Aug 2020

https://kutv.com/news/local/240k-utahns-info-breached-after-popular-gun-website-hacked

A popular gun exchange website is the victim of a security breach. (Photo: KUTV)

(KUTV) — A popular gun exchange website is the victim of a security breach.

Hackers reportedly stole 240,000 records from the Utah Gun Exchange, according to Forbes.

The information taken includes customer email addresses, login names and passwords.

The hacker is vowing to give away the data for free, according to Forbes.

The breach happened after hackers gained access to an Amazon cloud server.

Monday, August 17th 2020

SALT LAKE CITY (KUTV) — The Salt Lake County Clerk's Office has been getting regular calls from concerned citizens about mail-in ballots.

This video file cannot be played.(Error Code: 102630)

Cristina Flores reports. (KUTV)

Namely, when they will get their ballot for the November election -- with some worried their mail-in ballots may not be counted because of issues with the United States Postal Service (USPS).

To combat this problem, county clerks from across Utah are asking lawmakers, who will meet in a special session this week, to change a key law. That law says ballots can begin being mailed to registered voters 21 days before an election. The clerks are asking to change that to 28 days before the election, to allow more time for mail-in ballots to be delivered and processed by the post office.

Salt Lake County Clerk Sherrie Swensen talks to 2News reporter Cristina Flores about mail-in ballots and the 2020 election. (Adam Forgie, KUTV)

Utah Rep. Stephen Handy is sponsoring a bill to address the election during the COVID-19 pandemic, but he says it's unlikely the bill will give USPS more time to collect and process mail-in ballots. Handy tells 2News:

The bill doesn't break any new ground, but given the environment we are in, political messages coming from the White House, the legislatures is going to affirm that we have a safe election process, that there is incredible integrity in the process."

There will still be in-person voting as well as early voting, according to Handy, who says after an investigation there was no need to give the post office more time. "What we’ve been seeing in the investigation we’ve been doing is three weeks are adequate," Handy says.

Watch our full interview with Swensen here or below:

The bill will address some changes:

  • For the 2020 election only: requires each county provide in-person voting of some kind. If it's drive up, walk up, drive through, or a combination of the three, each county can make their own determination.
  • If the health risks are too high, Utah could shutdown in person voting, but the state would have to allow ballots to be accepted up to day of election or add more ballot boxes.

Salt Lake County Clerk Sherrie Swensen says:

I’m a little bit concerned because we don't know if [the post office] had staffing cuts or what their situation is. Also, in Salt Lake County, we will be sending out more ballots than we’ve ever sent. We have more registered voters than we’ve ever had. Comparatively in 2018, for the general election, we had 517,000 active registered voters. Right now, we have 562,000. By the November election, mailing date, we are probably going to have 580 ,000."

This comes during a nationwide slowdown of mail service following President Donald Trump's selection of a new postmaster a couple months ago.

In June, Louis DeJoy, a Republican donor and logistics company executive, took over as the new postmaster general and Trump tasked him with trying to make the Postal Service more profitable. DeJoy cut overtime, late delivery trips and other expenses that ensure mail arrives at its destination on time. The result has been a national slowdown of mail.

This has many lawmakers, including Republicans like Utah Senator Mitt Romney, criticizing those, including President Trump, who are attacking mail-in voting. The Democratic-run House on Sunday demanded that leaders of the U.S. Postal Service testify at an emergency oversight hearing Aug. 24 on mail delays.

Republican Utah Rep. Chris Stewart issued a statement showing his support for the post office, saying in a tweet:

I am committed to finding a path forward that ensures the longevity of the United States Postal Service. After decades of mismanagement and impossible mandates, Congress needs to step in an exercise its oversight role to place the USPS and its critical services back on a sustainable path."
Despite these challenges, vote by mail works in Utah. We have rigorous procedures in place to prevent voter fraud. America should follow Utah's lead."

Since Utah's post offices are expected to handle a record number of ballots, Swensen recommends returning your ballot as soon as you get it, if you plan to send it by mail. Folks are also encouraged to use ballot drop boxes, which are scattered around the valley.

Swensen says:

In Salt Lake County, we pay first class postage return, prepaid on the envelopes for voters to send their ballots back. But they also have the option to drop their ballot without mailing them. We have 20 drop boxes located throughout the county. They can drop their ballot 24-7. Those are picked up regularly by teams from my office. So they don’t even need to mail the ballot back."

Salt Lake County Clerk Sherrie Swensen talks to 2News about mail-in ballots and the 2020 election. (Adam Forgie, KUTV)

Swensen's office has not heard from local post offices about rumors of staffing cuts and cuts on mail sorting machines. She says:

I have not heard from our local post offices that there aren’t issues. We process them very diligently and the ballots will be counted. The only [problem] is if the signature doesn’t match or they forget to sign the affidavit. We always notify voters to remedy that."
