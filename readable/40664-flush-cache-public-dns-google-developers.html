Flush Cache | Public DNS | Google Developers

Caution: If you changed the DNS servers for your domain, by changing registrars or DNS hosting in the last few days, flush your main domain name first before you flush any subdomains. This ensures name server delegations are flushed before refresh attempts for subdomains get stale data from your old DNS servers.
Note: Domains with EDNS Client Subnet for geolocation cannot be flushed. More info.

Tip: If you see “Some servers did not respond to flush requests,” wait a minute or two before you try again. This prevents your request from being blocked due to rate limits on flush requests, so that your second or third try is more likely to succeed.
