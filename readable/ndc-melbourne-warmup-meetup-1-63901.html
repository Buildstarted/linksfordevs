NDC Melbourne Warmup Meetup #1

Reading time: 4-5 minutes
Posted here: 06 Jul 2020

NDC Melbourne takes place on 27-30 July 2020 - and this year, NDC Melbourne will be a completely online event. All the same great workshops, speakers, and conversations you've come to expect from NDC Conferences, but in an online format.

in our series of NDC Melbourne Warmup Meetups we're inviting some of the great speakers who will be appearing at NDC Melbourne. This week, Filip Ekberg, Adrienne Tacke, Ian Cooper and Amy Kapernick will be joining our panel hosted by Lars Klint to discuss "Using Tech in a Rapidly Changing World."

Using Tech in a Rapidly Changing World

The last few months has brought more change to the way we work, interact and communicate than the past decade. Video calling has become the norm for talking to friends, family and colleagues, social media has a much greater influence, and we have got a new appreciation for critical workers. And in the middle of it all is technology being pushed to the limit.

Join us for a panel discussion with Adrienne Tacke, Amy Kapernick and Filip Ekberg on how technology is changing the world faster than you can spell NDC.

A separate link to the webinar will be sent via email after registration.

About Lars Klint (Host)

Lars is an author, trainer, Microsoft MVP, community leader, aspiring Microsoft Azure expert and part-time classic car collector. He is heavily involved in the space of cloud computing services, especially Azure, loves HoloLens and mixed reality, and is a published author, solution architect and writer for numerous publications. He has been a part of the software development community for the past 20 years and co-organises the DDD Melbourne community conference, organises developer events with Microsoft, and also runs a part-time car restoration business. He has spoken at numerous technical events around the world and is an expert in Australian Outback Internet.

About Amy Kapernick (Panellist)

Amy wears many hats as a freelance developer, business owner and conference addict. She regularly shares her knowledge with her peers and the next generation of developers by mentoring, coaching, teaching and feeding into the tech community in many ways. Amy can be found volunteering her time with Fenders, ACS, SheCodes (formerly Perth Web Girls) and MusesJS (formerly NodeGirls). She also works as an evangelist for YOW! Conferences, is a Twilio Champion, Microsoft MVP and has been nominated for the WiTWA awards for the last 2 years. In her spare time Amy shares her knowledge and experience on her blogs and speaking at conferences. She has previously given keynotes at multiple events as well as speaking at several international conferences in the US and Europe.

About Ian Cooper (Panellist)

Polyglot Coding Architect in London, founder of #ldnug, speaker, tabletop gamer, geek. Tattooed, pierced, and bearded. The 'guv' on @BrighterCommand

About Adrienne Tacke (Panellist)

Adrienne Braganza Tacke is a Filipina software engineer, international speaker, and published author of the book Coding for Kids: Python. She is also a LinkedIn Learning instructor who specializes in Azure and Cloud Development courses. Currently, she is a Senior Developer Advocate for MongoDB where she happily educates and empowers developers to become great ones (perhaps using MongoDB in the process).

About Filip Ekberg (Panellist)

Filip is the author of C# Smorgasbord and a handful of Pluralsight courses, speaker at events and user groups around the globe, Microsoft and Xamarin MVP. He loves teaching fellow developers how to master C# by understanding important concepts such asynchronous programming, MSIL and much more.
