Facebook Stored Passwords in Plain Text For Years

by Author: Lindsey O'Donnell
Reading time: 4-5 minutes

The social media giant said that it is notifying users whose passwords it stored in plain text, which made them accessible for Facebook employees to view.

Hundreds of millions of Facebook user passwords have been stored in plain text for years, the social media giant acknowledged on Thursday.

KrebsOnSecurity, which first reported the news, said that specifically between 200 and 600 million passwords were stored in plain text as early as 2012, and were searchable by thousands of Facebook employees.
