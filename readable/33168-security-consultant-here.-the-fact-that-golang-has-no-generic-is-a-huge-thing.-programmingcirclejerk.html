Security consultant here. The fact that Golang has no generic is a huge thing. : programmingcirclejerk

We should not stray into the dark, but tempting, path that is Generics.

Generics is the snake in the grass, waiting for its pray to startle it.

Generics is the left-over land mine in Indochina, waiting for its pray to step on it.

Generics is the crocodile in the shallows, waiting for its pray to drink from it.

We might be lured by its attractive promises of reuse and higher abstractions.

A man might look at Generics with the lusty eyes that he would give his wife on their first night together. But the sweet embrace will soon turn into a chokehold.

The cacaphony of Generics must never take hold of our language. It is much more secure to explicitly write each data structure for the type we desire. Copy and paste bugs are a boogeyman, told to impressionable children.

We are not children, but men, and we shall walk tall towards the simple, almost naive but warming light that is a Go without Generics.
