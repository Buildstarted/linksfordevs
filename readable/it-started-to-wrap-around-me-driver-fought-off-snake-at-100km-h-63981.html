'It started to wrap around me': Driver fought off snake at 100km/h

by https://www.facebook.com/mattdennien
Reading time: 4-5 minutes
Posted here: 08 Jul 2020

https://www.brisbanetimes.com.au/national/queensland/it-started-to-wrap-around-me-driver-fought-off-snake-at-100km-h-20200707-p559ts.html

A central Queensland driver pulled over by police for speeding had "never been so happy" to see flashing lights after fighting off an eastern brown snake while trying to stop on a major road.

Police say 27-year-old Gladstone man "Jimmy" had been driving on the Dawson Highway south of the city about 5pm one night in mid-June when he noticed he was not alone.

"I'm driving along at 100, and I just started to brake," Jimmy recounted to police at the scene.

"The more I moved my legs… it just started to wrap around me. Its head just started striking at the (driver’s seat) chair, between my legs."

Jimmy then turned to a seatbelt and work knife he had in the ute to fight off and kill the reptile, while trying to stop safely and get out.

Certain he had been bitten by the now-dead snake — one of the deadliest in the world — he threw it in the tray and took off towards the nearest hospital.

A traffic officer clocked him travelling at 123km/h shortly after.

"Although the traffic officer had heard his fair share of excuses for speeding, he soon realised this was not just another colourful tale and promptly sought medical assistance," a police statement said.

Body camera footage of the June 15 ordeal shows a visibly shaken Jimmy trying to explain the situation to wary police.

"I think it has bitten me, it was in the car with me," he can be seen telling the officer. "You can feel my heart, mate."

On inspecting the back of the ute, the snake can be clearly seen under the officer's torchlight.

"He's pretty panicky, he's conscious and breathing at the moment," they can be heard to say. "But I need [paramedics] here immediately if you can."

On attending the scene, it was determined Jimmy had not been bitten by the snake, but he was in shock from the ordeal.

A Queensland police spokeswoman confirmed Jimmy was not issued a speeding fine.
