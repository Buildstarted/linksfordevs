August 18, 2018

I was wary of the new re-boot of MST3K because I am a long term fan of the original versions. But I was impressed by how they seemed to do their best to keep what was good about the old show while bringing it into the 21st century at the same time. Granted, getting used to the new characters was jarring at first: but it was the same when Weinstein was replaced by Murphy and Coniff, Hodgeson by Nelson, and Bellieu by Corbett. Eventually I got used to them all. In my opinion, the new characters are at least as funny as the old. The new "Gypsy" in particular is a huge improvement. All the production values are much improved (as you would expect after 30 years). I even love Kinga Forrester and TV's son of TV's Frank (Max) even though it's a stretch to believe that Clayton Forrester or TV's Frank ever had kids. So I just repeat to myself "it's just a show, I should really just relax"

Not nearly as funny as the original version with Joel and Mike as hosts. There were some good lines, but I'd like to see the writing improve if this continues into a second season. To be fair, it appears Netflix only gave the cast, crew and writers a very small window to get these episodes turned out. I remember watching a documentary on the original series where they would watch and riff the movies several times during the week to perfect the jokes and had time for rehearsals, etc. Perhaps they can work a deal to get more time per episode much like the original series. New cast was good and it was nice to see some of the original cast make appearances. All in all, (since I have almost all of the original series on DVD), I'm glad I got this set to continue collecting the DVDs and I'm glad they brought back the series.

March 14, 2019

Format: DVDVerified Purchase

I'm sure there were people who gave up on the original MST3K during the show's lackluster first season, and some viewers will be tempted to do the same this time around. Turns out the defining factor of the series reboot wasn't the audience's high hopes, it was the time crunch: how'd Joel and the cast feel when they found out that they had to record the entire 11th season overnight?

There was no time to fine-tune their characters: "Jonah Heston" is a blank slate, Crow and Servo are generic cartoon wiseguys, and as for Kinga Forrester and Max...well, there's always next season. A string of guest stars comes and goes, and the riffing is on a par with what you would've heard in the original series' first and second seasons: it's gettin' there, but it's still a work in progress.
