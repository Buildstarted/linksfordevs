Unity at GDC San Francisco 2019: Dates, Keynote, and Schedules | Unity

by Unity Technologies
Reading time: 39-50 minutes
Posted here: 27 Feb 2019

https://unity.com/gdc-2019

March 18—22, 2019 — San Francisco, United States

Experience the Keynote live

Update: Find Unity at GDC 2020 here.

Every year at GDC we make major announcements and debut our latest innovations. Be the first to witness our 2019 features, as we kick off the week with our Keynote and continue the conversation at booth #S227 on the expo floor.

This year will be our biggest yet, so don't miss it. Space is limited and is available on a first come, first served basis.

When:
Monday, March 18, 5:00 pm PST

Where:
846 Mission Street (Century Theaters entrance)

Can’t make it to the venue? No problem – for the first time ever we’re hosting Keynote parties in many of our offices. Join fellow artists, developers and gamers to watch the excitement as it unfolds live.

Check back soon for full location and RSVP details.

San Francisco Keynote Party

30 3rd St., San Francisco, CA

Bellevue Keynote Party

10400 NE 4th St, Suite 900, Bellevue, WA

Check our site regularly to learn more about our additional speaking sessions at GDC!

Developer Day

10:00 am – 6:30 pm | Moscone West, Room 3022

Our Developer Day at GDC is a full day of in-depth sessions dedicated to anyone creating with Unity, including programmers, technical artists, designers, animators, and storytellers. Join us to learn about new and upcoming technologies in Unity. 

Everyone Watching This Is Fired

2:00 – 2:30 pm | Room 3016, West Hall

Mike Acton, Principal Engineer, Unity Technologies

Attendees will take away a practical list of ways to improve their productivity and team performance. Mike will focus on data-oriented design, general problem-solving, understanding your tools, and team communication.

Toward Filmic Quality at 30 FPS: Real-Time Ray Tracing for Practical Game Engine Pipelines

4:00 – 5:00 pm | Room 2001, West Hall

Natasha Tatarchuk, Graphics Director, Unity Technologies

Attendees will learn about the pragmatic approaches for extending existing modern game engines to support real-time ray tracing without a complete rewrite. They will learn about how to achieve fast rendering on GPU through a set of techniques and how to improve visual quality with high-fidelity ray tracing algorithms. They will also learn important considerations for content creation targeting ray tracing and non-ray tracing rendering hardware with minimal impact to the authoring pipeline.

Evolving Mixed Reality: Designing on the Real World

11:20 am – 12:20 pm | Room 210, South Hall

Jono Forbes, Senior Software Engineer, Unity Technologies

In this talk, toolsmiths from Unity, Magic Leap, and Microsoft Research will discuss the challenges of enabling an MR future, and debate the approaches to overcoming them.

Mental Models and Systems for Spatial Computing

11:20 am – 12:20 pm | Room 202, South Hall

Timoni West, Director of XR Research, Unity Technologies

This talk covers the basics needed to start making a genuinely spatially aware application. Attendees will walk away aware of the vast amount of data and work needed to create apps that can fluidly shift states every time the user steps into a new room.

Super Powering Technical Artists Using Deep Learning

3:00 – 3:30 pm | Room 2001, West Hall

Cesar Romero, Principal Machine Learning Engineer, Unity Technologies

In this session, artists and developers will learn how machine learning can radically improve world-building by solving many time-consuming, manual tasks. In multiple demos and applications, you’ll see deep learning delivering delightful experiences, generating texture variations, enabling super-resolutions and much more.

Math for Game Developers: Inside Neural Networks

3:50 – 4:20 pm | Room 2006, West Hall

Michael Buttner, Principal Research Engineer, Unity Technologies

Neural networks are gaining attention because they can solve difficult problems, yet they are still not often used in real-time scenarios. Come learn how to leverage neural networks in performance-critical scenarios and how to write an efficient inference engine – without frameworks or math libraries –  using data-oriented design principles.

From The Trenches: Moving the Diversity Needle in Community Management

Deborah Oyeyinka, Social Media and Community Manager, Multiplay/Unity, and more

The goal is to have everyone who is interested make a commitment in the session to take decisive steps towards understanding more about why diversity in community management is important and valuable to your game's ecosystem.

Sessions at Unity Theater

All week long, join us at the Unity Theater (Expo floor, Booth S249) for great insight on a multitude of hot topics, such as the Data-Oriented Technology Stack (DOTS), the latest Unity 2019.1 features, best practices for mobile-game development from major studios and much more.

Unity Educator Breakfast

Start your day with fellow education leaders from around the world who are guiding the next generation of creators in interactive design and development. This is a unique opportunity to network,  share, and learn best practices for integrating interactive technology into programs across disciplines while nurturing student success.

Check out our sponsored sessions and learn about our Data-Oriented Technology Stack, Multiplayer, Graphics, Machine Learning and Artificial Intelligence.

Performance and Extensibility: A Look at the New Lightweight Render Pipeline

9:00 – 10:00 am | Room 3022, West Hall

André McGrail, Graphics Engineer

LWRP is getting out of preview. It brings improvements in performance, workflow, and extensibility over the Built-in pipeline. In this session you will learn the benefits of using LWRP, how to setup your project to use it and how to extend it to achieve custom rendering effects. We will demonstrate how to achieve some common game effects using LWRP and ShaderGraph.

Mastering the Apex of Scaling Game Servers

2:00 – 3:00 pm | Room 3022, West Hall

Larry Rye, Lead Solutions Engineer

Fresh off the back of scaling one of the fastest growing games of all time, Apex Legends, Multiplay takes to the stage to talk game server hosting. Joined by key members of the technical team from Respawn, Larry Rye will provide insight into Multiplay's tools for solving complex problems such as scaling your world across platforms and patching your game. Whatever stage of development you’re at, this talk will help you take another step towards the perfect player experience.

Connecting the DOTS – The Unity Data-Oriented Technology Stack

3:30 – 4:30 pm | Room 2020, West Hall

Mike Acton, Principal Engineer

In this session, you will learn about the components that make up the Data-Oriented Technology Stack and how they were applied in the Megacity sample project.

10:15 AM - 10:45 AM

Understanding Data-Oriented Design for Entity Component Systems

Unity's new ECS features enable huge performance improvements over traditional object-oriented ways of designing game systems, but data-oriented design is a much different way of thinking. Come to this session to learn how to think in a data-oriented way so you can take advantage of these new features.

10:45 AM - 11:15 AM

Converting Your Game to DOTS

Come to this session to learn what's involved in migrating existing game code to the new Data-Oriented Technology Stack (DOTS), which comprises the C# Job System, the Entity Component System (ECS), and the Burst Compiler. You'll also gain an understanding of the performance benefits you can expect from these systems. Finally, you'll learn when it makes sense to use them, and when to stick with the regular components you've used in the past.

11:15 AM - 11:45 AM

Leveraging DOTS-powered Physics

Havok will walk you through the journey of taking its AAA physics engine from C++ to the land of C# and the Data-Oriented Technology Stack (DOTS). Join this session to learn about the architecture and features now available in Unity Physics, see it in action, and learn how Unity Physics and Havok Physics can work together to maximize dynamism in your games.

11:45 AM - 12:15 PM

A Guide to Stadia Game Development

Can you build games in Unity? Then you can build games that will stream from the cloud. Attend this session to hear Zach Zarrow, Google Partner Engineer Lead, walk through how you can create games on Stadia, including many tools and workflows familiar to Unity developers. Attendees will be able to more easily navigate the learning resources, feel confident in getting started, and think about how their game development process can fit with Stadia.

1:00 PM - 1:30 PM

Using Vivox's Voice and Text Chat to Deliver the Best Multiplayer Experience

Vivox's voice and chat technology powers popular titles from PUBG to League of Legends. In this intro session, Vivox's Dave Verratti will show you how to implement voice/chat in your game to enable massive scale, cross-play, and immersive player experiences. And you can get started for free using the Vivox SDK.

1:30 PM - 2:00 PM

You Look Better as an Alien Cat – Multiuser MR with Unity & Magic Leap

Attend this session to find out how the Weta Workshop and Magic Leap teams quickly put together a competitive local multiuser mixed-reality (MR) experience on the ML-One, leveraging Unity's spatial mapping and powerful cross-platform AR support. Magic Leap's Thomas Hall will discuss problems they encountered that are unique to multiuser local experiences, as well as some solutions they came up with. He will also share key findings on network latency and bandwidth, simple techniques to let spectators enjoy the experience on other platforms, and more.

2:00 PM - 2:30 PM

Building Instant Experiences with Project Tiny

Ready to deliver games that don't require installation? Come to this session to learn how Project Tiny will let you connect with players instantly. Technical Evangelist Arturo Nunez will show you how to use Editor workflows and an ECS-based modular runtime to produce projects with blisteringly fast load times and very small transport sizes. You can use the initial release to deploy content via web technologies on mobile – ideal for powering playable ads or games in social and messaging apps. Arturo will also go over the Project Tiny roadmap and provide additional details on the open beta.

2:30 PM - 3:00 PM

Launch & Monetization Success with Panzerdog's Tacticool – A Case Study

Developing a F2P game? Want the inside scoop on how to achieve launch and monetization success? Attend this session to hear Alexey from Panzerdog talk about how they stayed ahead of the competition by using Unity's development, monetization, and acquisition services during their popular game's launches. He'll be covering soft-launch best practices, global-launch takeaways, and the basics of how to succeed as an indie developer.

3:30 PM - 4:00 PM

Introducing the New Prefab Workflow

Want a great introduction about Prefabs in Unity 2019? Attend this session! In just 25 minutes, seasoned game developer and technical evangelist Ciro Continisio will explain all the major aspects of our new Prefab workflow (for example, Nesting, Variants, and Prefab Isolation Edit mode). He'll also tell you about the new UI/UX elements and the reasoning behind them.

4:00 PM - 5:00 PM

Looking Ahead – The Unity 2019 Road Map

In this informative talk, Product Managers Arisa Scott and Will Goldstone will discuss our 2019 road map and give you a broad overview of Unity's 3-year vision. Join them to learn about updates for the Entity Component System (ECS), graphics, artist tooling, connected games and much more.

10:45 AM - 11:15 AM

Achieving High-Fidelity AR with the Lightweight Render Pipeline

Are you working on an AR project or want to get started on one? Be sure to attend this session led by Dan Miller, a Unity AR/VR Evangelist. He'll share the latest additions to our AR Foundation package, including Lightweight Render Pipeline (LWRP) support, AR Remote, and face-tracking. As well, he'll explain how to get started with AR Foundation and use some unique features only available in this multi-platform AR layer.

11:15 AM - 11:45 AM

Getting Started with the Megacity Demo

The Megacity demo is a futuristic cityscape – alive with flying vehicles, scores of highly detailed game objects and unique audio sources – that showcases what's possible with the Data-Oriented Technology Stack (DOTS) in Unity 2019.1. In this session, Unity Evangelist Mike Geig will give you an introduction to the project and show how we leveraged Unity's "Performance by default" to create a massive game world full of complex objects and simulations.

11:45 AM - 12:15 PM

Megacity on Mobile: How We Optimized It with Adaptive Performance

Have you seen our Megacity demo? With 4.5M mesh renderers, 200K unique objects per building, 100K audio sources, and 5K dynamic vehicles, running it on mobile was a major challenge. In this session, Unity's David Berger will reveal how the new Adaptive Performance feature, developed in partnership with Samsung, ensured a smoother mobile experience and greatly improved battery life.

1:00 PM - 1:30 PM

Blending Gameplay and Storytelling with Timeline – 2019 Improvements

If you're interested in both gameplay and storytelling, don't miss this session. Technical Evangelist Ciro Continisio will show how you can use the latest Timeline features – such as Markers and Signals, nested Control tracks, and root motion offsets – to produce compelling interactive experiences. He'll create a short animation in a game context, drive the cameras with Cinemachine, and integrate it all with the gameplay.

1:30 PM - 2:00 PM

Unleash Your Game Cameras with Cinemachine

Don't miss this chance to learn how Cinemachine, the most advanced virtual-camera system available, can help you do amazing things in any genre, including 3rd-person AAAs, 2D games, and cinematic cutscenes. Join Unity's Adam Myhill for this entertaining session so that you'll be creating your own sophisticated camera systems in no time.

2:00 PM - 2:30 PM

Beat Saber Live – Best Practices for Creating Award-Winning VR Experiences

Have you played Beat Saber? This groundbreaking VR game has sold over a million copies and won a slew of awards. Don't miss this chance to hear firsthand how Beat Games achieved their phenomenal success. Learn eye-opening tips & tricks for creating kick-ass VR from Beat Games' Jaroslav Beck (Co-Founder), Vladimir Hrincar (CTO and Indie Game Developer), and Michaela Dvorak (Head of Marketing).

2:30 PM - 3:00 PM

Building Beautiful Worlds with Unity's New Terrain Features

Designing landscapes for your games can be difficult. Unity's new Terrain system helps make that process a lot easier. In this session, Ashley will show you how to make your workflow faster and easier, while enabling you to design ever-more gorgeous landscapes.

You'll also see how the netcode of a fast-paced multiplayer shooter like this sample works.

We will also show how the netcode of a fast-paced multiplayer shooter works. Finally, we will discuss networked games at a high level and answer some of your questions as you begin planning your own multiplayer game with Unity.
