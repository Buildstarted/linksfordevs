For what's believed to be the first time in its illustrious history, Nottingham's centuries-old pub Ye Olde Trip to Jerusalem has been boarded up.

England's oldest inn, once a pit stop for crusader knights, is where King Richard the Lionheart and his men are likely to have gathered before journeying to Jerusalem in 1189.

Since then it's weathered the Black Death and world wars but it's taken the coronavirus pandemic for the windows and doors to be barricaded.

The security measure has been taken by the brewery Greene King.

A simple hand-written banner hangs from the pub from staff saying a huge thank you to the NHS and key workers, with the hashtags stay home and stay safe, plus a rainbow.

A spokesperson for Greene King said: “We have taken the decision to board up a number of our pubs across the city for the duration of their closure due to the coronavirus pandemic.

"Although no stock or cash is left on site, it is still of vital importance that we do all we can to protect these much-loved pubs so they are there for the people of Nottingham to enjoy when we are able to reopen."

The Trip isn't the only pub to be boarded up. The Bell Inn, in Angel Row, and the Water's Edge, in Castle Bridge Road, also have wooden boards at the windows.

Nottingham councillor, Sam Webster, portfolio holder for finance, growth and the city centre, said it's a sight he never thought he'd see.

"While we never like to see boarded up business premises and never expected to see Ye Olde Trip to Jerusalem boarded up, we can take heart that this is only a temporary security measure as those in the retail, leisure and hospitality sectors protect their businesses during the pandemic.

"What is heartening is the thoughtfulness of staff at the Trip in providing a message to support for the NHS staff and key workers who are working so hard to keep people safe and well, which is a wonderful gesture from an iconic Nottingham business.

“We’ve pulled together a directory of support from Nottingham City Council, Central Government and local partners who can support businesses during the Coronavirus outbreak.

"We are committed to helping our local businesses survive at this difficult time. When normality does eventually return I’m sure that we’ll all be showing our support for Nottingham’s fantastic local businesses.”
