My first program was on a VTech Toy Laptop from Toys R Us when I was 7 or so. It had a really basic BASIC interpreter on it with an 8 line LED display with something like 14 characters per row.

I just followed the book and made a program to ask you for a password before letting you pic some random number 1-10.

I thought I was a Wizard with magical powers at first.

Even when I got my first 386 when I wasn't much older than that and configured the sound blaster to talk on boot up. I thought I was a Grand Wizard. They were like magical black boxes to me and I was doing magic.

Fast forward 25 years and I basically know so much crap now it's boring, nothing is particularly challenging. Computers were so interesting to me as a child that I spent my life sucking every ounce of wonder out of them that I possibly could.

Now it's kind of boring.... Build a login form, do SSO auth, make a searchbox, do a single page app, build a date picker, or w/e "yawn" and I can build computers from parts, and I can even write ASM and inject it into other applications and hook asm instructions etc etc. Base 2, Base 10, Base 16, doesn't matter.

/yawn

Man, I wish I could go back to thinking they were magical black boxes.

Somehow, someway, I need to move on from web development and do something interesting, like programming drones, or arduinos, etc etc etc. I've got arduinos, not particularly interesting either.

What I think I really want to do is learn electrical engineering to the point that I understand electrical circuit diagrams and can design my own PCB's so I can move my arduino prototypes to something more concrete. I understand the basics, I know what the core basic electrical components do and how to use them, I just need to learn more in terms of what an electrical engineering degree would teach you.
