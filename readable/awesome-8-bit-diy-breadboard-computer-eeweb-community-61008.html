Here’s the most amazing 8-bit DIY computer implemented using a bunch of simple logic chips — along with a cornucopia of flashing LEDs — mounted on a cluster of breadboards.

I stand in awe. As I’ve said many times before (and as I will doubtless say many times again), “Show me a flashing LED, and I will show you a man drooling.” Well, I’ve just seen the most amazing 8-bit DIY computer implemented using a bunch of simple logic chips — along with a cornucopia of flashing LEDs — mounted on a cluster of breadboards.


8-bit DIY computer (Source: Ben Eater)

According to his website, the architect of this bodacious beauty, Ben Eater, is currently focused on creating educational videos for his YouTube channel and exploring other ways to maximize human potential through education online. In his spare time, Ben enjoys taking airplanes for a spin (real ones, not models). I myself like taking moonlit walks on the beach in the rain while eating bacon sandwiches, but we digress…

Take a look at this video to see Ben’s beauty in action, and you can also visit the 8-bit computer portion of Ben’s website to discover more.

I don’t know about you, but I am sitting here lusting over this little rascal. It’s almost a work of art. I can so imagine it in a glass-fronted wooden case mounted on my office wall. You can purchase the kit in the form of modules (clock module, registers and ALU module, RAM and program counter module…), or you can save $20 by purchasing the complete 8-bit computer kit for $269.96.

Now, on the one hand, $269.96 is a fair chunk of change. On the other hand, there are a lot of breadboards and other “stuff” here, plus a huge amount of work has gone into architecting this little beauty and creating the associated videos.

And then there’s the educational value. Anyone who builds this kit will come out the other end knowing a heck of a lot about how computers work at the most fundamental level. This would also make a great project for parents and kids to build and learn together. Hmm, being that I’m a big kid at heart, I wonder if I can persuade my wife (Gina the Gorgeous) to buy me one for Christmas…
