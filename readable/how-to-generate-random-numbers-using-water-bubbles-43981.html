How to generate random numbers using water bubbles

by Jason Fenech
Watching time: 1 minute
Posted here: 12/10/2019

https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=LUKy0Gaxn1g


BubblesRNG - This was my final project I submitted as part of my reading the B.Sc. Computing and Information Systems degree at Goldsmith, University of London. The camera take snapshots of the water surface at regular intervals and detects bubbles (circles) depending on the user-set Hough transform parameters. Bubbles are produced using two aquarium water pumps, the generation and location of which is assumed to be random; not replayable. The x,y location of the bubbles captured - the ones that fit the Hough transform parameters set - are saved as binary with the option to first whiten or condition the data stream using all or a combination of AES-128 encryption, XOR, and shuffling. True randomness was determined by running the streams of random bits through toolkits such as ENT, NIST-STS, DieHard and Rabigete. My project was voted best submission for 2016!
