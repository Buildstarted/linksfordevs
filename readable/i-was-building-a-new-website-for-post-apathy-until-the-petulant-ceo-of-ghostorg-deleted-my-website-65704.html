I was building a new website for Post Apathy - until the petulant CEO of Ghost.org deleted my website

by Ahmed
Reading time: 4-6 minutes
Posted here: 15 Aug 2020

https://postapathy.substack.com/p/i-was-building-a-new-website-for

Edit: I'm now in the process of trying to resolve this issue and see whether there's a possibility of restoring Post Apathy. My aim here isn't to feud or do beef-as-marketing, but to restore my beautiful website and ensure rights and freedoms aren't arbitrarily taken away.

Dear subscribers,

In July, I promised to begin a trial schedule to post fortnightly newsletters, as opposed to the monthly schedule that I had going previously. Today was meant to be the day where the next newsletter was meant to be sent out, but I decided to delay it as I had been busy building a website via Ghost.org and I wanted to start posting there as soon as it was built, so that there was minimum interruption in your access to Post Apathy content.

I spent hours designing it, getting content on to it, and was just about to spend hundreds of dollars on upgrading it. It was looking beautiful and I was quite proud of it!

However, I woke up this morning and saw an e-mail in my inbox:

I found this to be shady. Some people do not check their email for a good week or two, and yet I was expected to stay on top of them or risk an automatic “upgrade” from $36 to $99 for having an extra staff user on my website? I had forgotten about this, but why not just block me from adding them? I sent this fairly polite email to Ghost’s customer support:

I also decided to tweet about it - because that’s what people do, right?

John O’Nolan, Ghost’s CEO, responds like this (of course, he quickly deleted these tweets):

Being pretty shocked at this treatment, I think little of it. I start to wonder whether I should go through with upgrading the website to a full plan or not. I don’t like being fucked with, but at the same time I’m not that petty, so I decide to leave it because he’s clearly disturbed.

15 minutes later, as I am working on my website, I get kicked out.

I thought it was an internet issue - nope. I received this email from the CEO of Ghost:

Because I complained about a certain practice on Twitter, the CEO of a company decided to arbitrarily revoke my access to something I had paid for and pull down my website. I have lost hours of work within a few minutes due to a petty CEO’s decision to arbitrarily punish a paying customer because of a minor complaint. This wasn’t the “the future of publishing” I was expecting. I had only high praise for Ghost, as anyone can attest by my Twitter timeline. The moment I had a complaint, everything was gone.

I want to apologise to my subscribers for not delivering new content this week, and for failing to deliver on the promise of a new website. Right now, I’m really angry and I’m not exactly sure what to do next. Ghost had (until today) offered a smooth UI and excellent customer care, compared to Substack which have repeatedly ignored emails, left bugs running, and haven’t been clear with their payment system. Now, not only is my website gone, but it’s clear to me that the sheer capriciousness of this move by Ghost’s CEO John O’Nolan means Ghost is unsafe for anyone who dares to voice what is an otherwise-ordinary complaint. If you complain, you could risk losing your website in much the same way I did, in a matter of minutes.

I may continue to publish on Substack after all. Even if they ignore their customers, they don’t arbitrarily wipe their websites and kick them out. I may try and find another platform that I can trust not to act in a petulant and unprofessional manner. All I can say is that if you are considering building a website or know someone who is - dont be naive like me and recommend Ghost. Avoid Ghost at all costs, or it will cost you.

I’m going to try and gather myself to get Post Apathy back on track. This is a setback, but the demand is high and I really enjoy compiling the newsletters and writing the original essays, so things will get back to normal soon. I want to apologise again and thank you for your patience.
