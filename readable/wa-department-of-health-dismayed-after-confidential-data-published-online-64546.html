WA Department of Health 'dismayed' after confidential data published online

Reading time: 3 minutes
Posted here: 20 Jul 2020

https://www.abc.net.au/news/2020-07-20/wa-health-department-investigates-confidential-data-breach/12475310

The WA Government has confirmed confidential data from the state's Department of Health was made publicly available on a website after it was distributed over a third-party paging service.

Key points:

  • A paging system was being used to send confidential health data
  • The system, run by a third party, has now been suspended
  • WA Health says its own data systems have not been breached

According to a report from Channel 9, a website was set up which provided confidential information about Western Australian patients and doctors, including those with suspected COVID-19 infection.

In a statement, a spokesman for the WA Department of Premier and Cabinet said action was taken as soon as the Government was alerted to the data breach.

He said the breach of confidential data was associated with the use of a third-party pager service by the Department of Health.

"The Department of Health immediately contacted the vendor and asked that the paging component of its service be ceased until the issue is addressed," the statement said.

"The paging service provider has indicated their services have not been compromised and is working with the telecommunications provider."

Similar issues have occurred in the United Kingdom, where radio enthusiasts have livestreamed sensitive medical information that was being sent via pagers in the National Health Service.

In 2019, the UK's Department of Health and Social Care said it would require NHS trusts to phase out pagers by the end of 2021.

Department says patient confidentiality of 'utmost importance'

The WA Department of Health released its own statement, saying it was "dismayed" by the data breach.

"Patient confidentiality is of utmost importance to the Department of Health, and it is dismayed that confidential information may have made it into the public domain," the statement said.

The spokesman said the State Government and WA Police were attempting to have the website shut down as quickly as possible.

"The Department and HSS can confirm that there has not been a breach of health data sources.

"These systems remain secure."
