Man uses car to cook roast pork in scorching heat

As Western Australia continues to swelter in 50C temperatures, a Perth man has successfully roasted a pork in his car in the extreme heat.

Stu Pengelly posted video of the experiment to his Facebook page on Friday, showing just how hot the inside of a car can get in the scorching heat.

"I cooked 1.5kg pork roast inside an old Datsun Sunny for 10hrs on a 39 degree day," Mr Pengelly said.

Mr Pengelly gave updates on the temperature as the day progressed, claiming the mercury reached 81C inside the car at 1pm.

He included photos of the finished product, showing the roast pork sliced and successfully cooked throughout.

Mr Pengelly noted that the cooking experiment showed just how hot the inside of a car can get in the Australian summer and warned against leaving children or pets in the car unattended.

"Do not leave anyone or anything precious to you in a hot car â€“ not for a minute," he wrote.

Heatwave conditions scorched the state last week, sweeping through inland Australia across to eastern states and territories this week.

The Australian Weather Bureau estimated that yesterday, Australia experienced its hottest day on record with several states and territories reaching into the 40s simultaneously.
