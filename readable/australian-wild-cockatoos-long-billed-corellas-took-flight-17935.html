Australian wild cockatoos (long-billed corellas) took flight

by Portia Shao
Watching time: 0 minutes

https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=ATFloRpbQHA


Not as many as the PBS special, but we were just driving down a road! A flock of long-billed corollas took flight from the eucalyptus trees by the road when we drove from Halls Gap to Melbourne. We stopped and they flew overhead, making a great cacophony of noise with their wings and also talking. It was one of the coolest experiences I had with a bunch of birds. I figure there were between 200-500 of them at least.
